Meeting: Pricing Model Review
Date: 2025-09-09
Attendees: Sara (CEO), Léa (PM), David (Eng Lead), Yann (Sales)

Sara: We need to settle on the pricing model before the sales team starts the
outbound push next week. Yann, where are we?

Yann: The market is split. Half our competitors do per-seat, half do
usage-based. Our prospects keep asking which one we are, and right now I don't
have a clean answer.

Léa: Per-seat is simpler to sell. Procurement understands it. Usage-based has
better expansion economics but the sales cycle gets longer because finance teams
hate variable bills.

David: From an engineering perspective, usage-based requires us to build
metering infrastructure now, not later. That's three to four weeks of work we
haven't budgeted.

Sara: What's the customer feedback been?

Yann: Mid-market wants per-seat. Enterprise is fine with either, but a few of
the bigger prospects actually prefer usage-based because they can start small
and grow without a procurement cycle every time.

Sara: Okay. Let's go per-seat for launch. We hit the easier sales motion first,
get to revenue faster, and we can layer usage-based as a second tier in Q2 once
we have real data on how customers consume the product.

Léa: Tiers?

Sara: Three tiers. Starter, Growth, Enterprise. Yann, you and Léa work out the
exact pricing this week. Decision: per-seat pricing for launch, three tiers,
usage-based deferred to Q2.

Yann: Understood. I'll have a draft pricing sheet by Friday.

David: I'll deprioritize the metering work then.

Sara: Yes. Don't build it until we commit to Q2 usage-based.
