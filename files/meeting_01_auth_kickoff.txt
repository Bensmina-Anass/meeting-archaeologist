Meeting: Auth System Kickoff
Date: 2025-09-02
Attendees: Sara (CEO), Marc (CTO), Léa (PM), David (Eng Lead)

Sara: Alright, let's lock down the auth approach for the B2B platform. We've been
going back and forth for two weeks. I want a decision today.

Marc: I've looked at the options. We have three realistic paths: build it ourselves
on top of Passport, use Auth0, or use Clerk. My recommendation is Auth0.

David: Why not build? We have the team for it.

Marc: Because we're a 12-person company and auth is not our differentiator. Every
hour we spend on password reset flows is an hour we're not spending on the actual
product. Auth0 gives us SSO, SAML, MFA, and audit logs out of the box. Our
enterprise customers will demand all of that within six months.

Léa: What's the cost story?

Marc: Auth0 starts at around 240 dollars a month for the B2B essentials tier, and
it scales with active users. By year two we're probably looking at 2k a month,
maybe more. Clerk is cheaper but their SAML support is weaker and that's a
dealbreaker for enterprise.

Sara: And migration risk if we change later?

Marc: Real but manageable. We abstract the auth layer behind our own service so
swapping providers is contained. I'd rather pay the Auth0 tax now than burn three
engineers for two months building something that will be worse.

David: Fine, I'm convinced. Auth0 it is.

Sara: Good. Decision: we go with Auth0 for authentication. Marc owns the
integration. Target is to have it live in staging by end of September. Léa, can
you make sure the enterprise customer requirements doc reflects what Auth0 gives
us for free?

Léa: On it.

Sara: Anything else on auth? No? Moving on.
