Meeting: On-Call & Observability
Date: 2025-10-28
Attendees: Marc (CTO), David (Eng Lead), Karim (Backend Eng)

Marc: We had two incidents last week that nobody noticed for over an hour. We
don't have a real on-call setup. Time to fix it.

David: What we have now is whoever happens to see the Slack alert. That's not
on-call, that's hope.

Karim: I propose PagerDuty for paging, Datadog for metrics and logs, Sentry
for error tracking. Datadog is expensive but it's the one tool I won't
compromise on — the alternatives are noticeably worse.

Marc: Cost?

Karim: Datadog at our scale is around 1.5k a month and it grows with hosts.
PagerDuty is 21 dollars per user per month, so call it 250 a month for the
team. Sentry is 80 a month on the team plan.

David: Rotation?

Marc: Weekly, four engineers in the rotation — David, Karim, me, and the new
senior backend hire when they start. One primary, one secondary.

Karim: SLOs?

Marc: 99.5 percent for the API in year one. We're not promising 99.9 until we
have the maturity to back it. Page on SLO burn rate, not on individual error
spikes. Otherwise the on-call gets paged constantly and learns to ignore
alerts.

David: Runbooks?

Karim: I'll write the first three for the most common incident types this
week. Everyone in the rotation contributes one runbook before their first
shift.

Marc: Decision: PagerDuty plus Datadog plus Sentry, weekly rotation of four
engineers, 99.5 percent API SLO, alert on burn rate not spikes, runbooks
mandatory before first shift. Karim owns tooling setup, David owns rotation
schedule and runbook standards.

David: Compensation for on-call?

Marc: Flat 200 euros per week of primary. We'll revisit when the team grows.
