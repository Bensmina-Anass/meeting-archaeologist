Meeting: Cloud & Deployment Strategy
Date: 2025-09-30
Attendees: Marc (CTO), David (Eng Lead), Karim (Backend Eng), Sara (CEO)

Marc: Today we lock the cloud strategy. We've been deferring this and it's
starting to block decisions downstream. Karim, you led the analysis.

Karim: Right. The question is single-cloud versus multi-cloud, and which
provider. I evaluated AWS, GCP, and a multi-cloud setup using Kubernetes as
the abstraction layer.

David: And?

Karim: Single-cloud on AWS. Strong recommendation.

Sara: Walk me through the reasoning. I keep hearing from advisors that
multi-cloud is the safer bet.

Karim: It's safer in theory and worse in practice. Multi-cloud doubles our
operational surface area. We'd need to maintain two sets of IAM policies, two
networking models, two billing accounts, two on-call playbooks. For a team of
twelve, that's a tax we cannot afford. The "vendor lock-in" risk people quote
is real but it's a five-year problem, not a year-one problem.

Marc: AWS specifically because?

Karim: Three reasons. One, our team has the most AWS experience — David and I
have both shipped production systems on it. Two, our enterprise customers
overwhelmingly run on AWS, and being in the same cloud simplifies private
networking, PrivateLink, data residency conversations. Three, RDS, the
database we already committed to, is best-in-class on AWS.

David: I want to flag one thing. We are explicitly saying no to GCP for now,
even though their data and ML tooling is better. That is a real tradeoff.

Marc: Acknowledged. If we ever build a serious ML pipeline we revisit. But
that's a year-two problem at the earliest.

Sara: Okay. Decision: we are single-cloud on AWS. No multi-cloud, no GCP
workloads, no Kubernetes-as-abstraction. We commit fully to AWS-native
services where they make sense — RDS, ECS, S3, CloudFront. Karim owns the
account structure.

Karim: I'll have the org structure and baseline accounts set up within two
weeks.

Marc: And David, this means the deployment tooling discussion is closed.
ECS with Fargate, not Kubernetes.

David: Confirmed. I'll kill the EKS spike.
