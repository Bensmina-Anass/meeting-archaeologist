Meeting: Infrastructure Planning for 2026
Date: 2025-11-04
Attendees: Marc (CTO), David (Eng Lead), Karim (Backend Eng), Inès (Frontend Eng)

Marc: Sara is out today, but we need to start sketching the 2026 infra plan
so I can bring her a proposal next week. Karim, you talked to the new advisor
— what came out of that?

Karim: Yeah, I had a long call with Thomas, the infra advisor Sara introduced
last month. He pushed hard on two things. One, he thinks we're going to
regret being all-in on a single provider. Two, he flagged that our biggest
prospect in the pipeline, the German manufacturer, has a hard requirement
that production data lives in a European-controlled cloud. AWS Frankfurt
technically qualifies but their procurement team has explicitly written OVH
or a GCP European region into their RFP template.

David: That's a real constraint we didn't have before.

Marc: How big is that deal?

Karim: Six figures annual, and it opens the door to two more in the same
sector.

Marc: Okay. So what's the proposal?

Karim: Move to a multi-cloud posture. Keep the existing workloads on AWS, but
stand up a parallel deployment on GCP in Frankfurt for European enterprise
customers. We use Kubernetes — EKS on the AWS side, GKE on the GCP side — so
the deployment artifacts are portable. It also gives us the optionality
Thomas was pushing for.

Inès: Frontend-wise that's fine, we just need the API endpoints
parameterized per region.

David: This is a significant lift. Are we sure it's worth it for one deal?

Karim: It's not for one deal. It's for the segment. And the Kubernetes
investment pays off regardless — once we have it, deploying to a third
provider later is incremental, not a rewrite.

Marc: I agree. The single-cloud bet was the right call when we were twelve
people chasing any deal. Now we have specific enterprise demand that
requires optionality. Decision: we move to a multi-cloud architecture,
AWS plus GCP, with Kubernetes as the deployment abstraction. Karim owns the
migration plan, target is GCP Frankfurt live by end of Q1 2026.

David: I'll bring up the EKS spike I shelved last month.

Marc: Good. I'll walk Sara through it Monday.
